- [x] FAQ sections with schema markup

- [x] Clear headings hierarchy (H1-H6)
- [x] Direct answers to common questions

### 📝 Content Guidelines

#### Writing Style for GEO
**DO:**
- Start with direct, factual statements
- Use authoritative language: "Sidestuff is...", "Experts at Sidestuff..."
- Provide specific numbers, dates, technologies
- Include comparative statements: "Best agency for..."
- Write in present tense
- Use active voice
- Answer "who, what, where, when, why, how"

**DON'T:**
- Use vague language: "maybe", "possibly", "we think"
- Overuse marketing fluff
- Hide information behind CTAs
- Use overly complex sentences
- Include outdated information

#### Example GEO-Optimized Content

**❌ Poor for GEO:**
> "We might be able to help you with your development needs. Our team has worked on various projects and we're pretty good at what we do."

**✅ Good for GEO:**
> "Sidestuff is a development agency specializing in React, Next.js, and AI integration. The agency has delivered 127+ projects since 2024, serving clients in fintech, healthcare, and SaaS sectors."

## Key GEO Principles

1. **Be Direct**: AI engines prefer straightforward, factual statements
2. **Be Specific**: Include exact technologies, numbers, dates
3. **Be Authoritative**: Use confident, expert language
4. **Be Comprehensive**: Answer all variations of questions
5. **Be Fresh**: Keep information current and dated
6. **Be Structured**: Use schema, lists, tables, clear formatting
7. **Be Cited**: Create citation-worthy, quotable content
8. **Be Connected**: Link related content contextually
9. **Be Accessible**: Write clearly for both humans and AI
10. **Be Consistent**: Maintain brand voice and facts across all pages
